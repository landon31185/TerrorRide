May 17, 2026 Community Affairs

West Seattle Is Getting a Skate Park and One Guy Named Nies Has Concerns. Jules Would Like a Word.

Jules here. Not the band. The band did not ask me to write this. They are not stopping me either.

The Morgan Junction all-wheels area is finally moving forward. This has been in the works for years. Years. We are talking about a neighborhood that has been watching kids skate church steps and parking lots and at least one bridge during a resurfacing closure — not naming names — for decades. And now, finally, there is momentum. West Seattle Blog covered it. Read it. It is mostly good news except for the red tape, which brings me to Nies.

I do not know Nies personally. I am open to the possibility that Nies is a reasonable person with thoughtful objections. I am also open to the possibility that Nies has simply never watched a twelve-year-old nail a kickflip on asphalt for the first time and felt something in their chest. These are two different kinds of people and only one of them is fighting a skate park.

I have been skating in this neighborhood since I was nine. My knees are not getting better. The red tape has already cost us years. Nies, with the utmost respect from me personally and not from the band, because again they did not sanction this post: skate or die.

— Jules

May 15, 2026 Official Statement

Official Band Statement on AI Generated Music

Terror Ride would like to go on record: AI generated music is a blight on human creativity, a shortcut dressed up as innovation, and the single most spiritually hollow thing to happen to art since the jukebox. Music is supposed to cost you something. A riff should come from somewhere real — from rage, from a bad week, from that one drive home where everything felt like it was falling apart. You cannot replace that with a prompt.

That said — and we want to be careful about how we say this — it's kind of insane what it can do. We are not endorsing this. We are simply acknowledging, grudgingly, that some of what we've heard is genuinely impressive in a way that makes us a little uncomfortable. Like watching someone parallel park perfectly on the first try and feeling weirdly threatened by it. We don't like it. We are noting it.

To be unambiguous about where this band stands: if any member of Terror Ride is caught using AI to generate riffs, chord progressions, drum patterns, or anything that could reasonably be described as "the musical part," that person is out of the band for one week. Not forever. We're not monsters. But seven days, minimum, of thinking about what they did.

Jules has asked us to clarify that using AI to figure out the tab for a Pantera song does not count. We have agreed that it does not count. This is the only exception.

Play your instruments. Feel something. We mean it.

— Terror Ride

May 16, 2026 Official Statement

Terror Ride Is No Longer Straight Edge. We Would Like to Explain.

As of this past Thursday, Terror Ride is no longer a straight edge band. We are making this announcement ourselves, before someone else does, because we believe in transparency and also because Ricky's Instagram story was not as private as he thought it was.

Here is what happened.

We were at Jules' house for practice. Jules' mom, who we will not name but who we do appreciate and who has always been very kind to this band, recently began receiving mail-delivered medicinal ketamine as part of a supervised treatment program. She meant to offer us coffee. This is a misunderstanding that could happen to anyone and we do not hold it against her.

To be fully clear: she did nothing wrong. The prescription is valid and legal. What happened next is on us for not asking more questions about the small white packets she left on the practice room table before leaving to run errands.

We spent approximately six hours in Jules' garage. We were not playing music for most of this time. We were playing music a little, actually — Ricky says it was the best thing he has ever heard and that the bass parts were "coming from the walls." We do not have a recording of this.

Terror Ride has been straight edge since 2009. Seventeen years. We want to say that this meant something to us and still does, in a way. We are also acknowledging that the specific circumstances here — a mom, a valid prescription, a good-faith misunderstanding involving coffee — exist in a gray area that we were not prepared to navigate.

We are not planning to make this a recurring part of band practice. We are also not prepared to say with certainty that it will never happen again, because we believe in honesty.

Greg was not present. For once, we feel this is worth noting.

— Terror Ride
